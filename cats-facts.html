facts about cats

  1. A house cat's genome is 95.6 percent tiger, and they share many behaviors with their jungle ancestors, says Layla Morgan Wilde, a cat behavior expert and the founder of Cat Wisdom 101. These behaviors include scent marking by scratching, prey play, prey stalking, pouncing, chinning, and urine marking.
  2. Cats are believed to be the only mammals who don't taste sweetness.
  3. Cats can jump up to six times their length.
  4. Cats have 230 bones , while humans only have 206.
  5. Cats have an extra organ that allows them to taste scents on the air, which is why your cat stares at you with her mouth open from time to time
  6. Cats have whiskers on the backs of their front legs, as well.
  7. Cats have nearly twice the amount of neurons in their cerebral cortex as dogs.
  8. Cats have the largest eyes relative to their head size of any mammal.
  9. Cats' rough tongues can lick a bone clean of any shred of meat .
  10. Cats use their long tails to balance themselves when they're jumping or walking along narrow ledges.
  11. Cats use their whiskers to “feel” the world around them in an effort to determine which small spaces they can fit into. A cat's whiskers are generally about the same width as its body. (This is why you should never, EVER cut their whiskers.)
  12. Cats walk like camels and giraffes: They move both of their right feet first, then move both of their left feet. No other animals walk this way
  13. Some cats can swim.
  14. A cat's average lifespan increased by a year over the span of time between 2002 and 2012, according to a study by Banfield Pet Hospital.
  15. According to The Huffington Post, cats typically sleep for 12 to 16 hours a day.
  16. Cats' purring may be a self-soothing behavior, since they make this noise when they're ill or distressed, as well as when they're happy.
  17. Cats live longer when they stay indoors.
  18. Cats will refuse an unpalatable food to the point of starvation.
  19. Despite popular belief, many cats are actually lactose intolerant.
  20. Female cats have the ability to get pregnant when they are only 4 months old!
  21. Grapes and raisins, as well as onions, garlic, and chives, are all extremely harmful foods for cats. Grapes and raisins can cause kidney failure — although the reasoning behind that isnt clear. Meanwhile, onions, garlic, and chives wreak havoc on your cats gastrointestinal system and can cause anemia.
  22. Male cats who have been fixed need fewer calories to maintain their weight.
  23. A cat with a question-mark-shaped tail is asking, “Want to play?”
  24. According to Wilde, a slow blink is a “kitty kiss”. This movement shows contentment and trust.
  25. Cats have a unique “vocabulary” with their owner — each cat has a different set of vocalizations, purrs and behaviors.
  26. Cats have up to 100 different vocalizations — dogs only have 10.
  27. Cats find it threatening when you make direct eye contact with them.
  28. Cats mark you as their territory when they rub their faces and bodies against you, as they have scent glands in those areas.
  29. When cats hit you with retracted claws, they're playing, not attacking.
  30. When dogs wag their tails, they may be expressing happiness. But this isn't the case for cats! When your cat wags her tail, it's her way of warning you that you are getting on her last nerve.
  31. When your cat sticks his butt in your face, he is doing so as a gesture of friendship.
  32. Whiskers are also good indicators of a cat's mood. When a cat is scared, he put his whiskers back. But when a cat is in hunting mode, he puts his whiskers forward.
  33. Your cat drapes its tail over another cat,your dog, or you as a symbol of friendship.
  34. Cats are very fussy about their water bowls; some prefer to ignore their bowls entirely in favor of drinking from the sink faucet.
  35. Cats love to sleep in laundry baskets, too, because they're basically hiding places with peep holes.
  36. Cats often attack your ankles when they're bored.
  37. Certain cats go crazy for foods you wouldn't expect, like olives, potato chips, and the hops in beer.
  38. For some reason, cats really dislike citrus scents.
  39. If you can't find your cat, you should look in a box or a bag, as these are some of their favorite hiding spots!
  40. Many cats like to lick their owner's freshly washed hair.
  41. Some cats love the smell of chlorine.
  42. A green cat was born in Denmark in 1995. Some people believe that high levels of copper in the water pipes nearby may have given his fur a verdigris effect.
  43. It turns out that Abraham Lincoln was a crazy cat president! He had four cats that lived in the White House with him.
  44. Maria Assunta left her cat, Tomasso, her entire $13 million fortune when she died in 2011.
  45. President Bill Clinton's cat, Socks, was a media darling during the Clinton administration and was said to receive more letters than the President himself.
  46. A cat's learning style is about the same as a 2- to 3-year-old child.
  47. A group of kittens is called a “kindle”.
  48. Cats dream, just like people do.
  49. Cats can drink sea water in order to survive. (In case you're wondering, we can't.)
  50. Cats have contributed to the extinction of 33 different species.
  51. Cats were first brought to the Americas in colonial times to get rid of rodents.
  52. Each cat's nose print is unique, much like human fingerprints.
  53. It's not uncommon to see cats in food stores in big cities as a form of free — and adorable — pest control.
  54. Kittens in the same litter can have more than one father. This is because the female cat releases multiple eggs over the course of a few days when she is in heat.
  55. Most world languages have a similar word to describe the “meow” sound.
  56. Some 700 million feral cats live in the United States, and many shelters run trap-neuter-release programs to stem the population growth.
  57. Studies suggest that domesticated cats first appeared around 3600 B.C.
  58. The first known cat video was recorded in 1894.
  59. There are about 88 million pet cats in the United States, which makes them the most popular pet in the country!
  60. White Cats with blue eyes are prone to deafness.
